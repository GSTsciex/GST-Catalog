Driving Service Excellence through Competencies

Competency-based learning is an approach to teaching and learning that is often used in learning tangible skills. It differs from other non-related approaches in that the function of learning is extremely defined to the particular skill. In comparison to the traditional course or module an individual skill and learning outcome, is bundled in as a competency and assessed holistically as one single skill.

In a Competency-based learning, learners work on one competency at a time, which is likely a small component of a larger learning goal. The student is evaluated on the individual competency, and only once they have mastered it do they move on to others. After that, higher or more complex competencies are learned to a degree of mastery and isolated from other topics. Another common component of Competency-based learning is the ability to skip learning modules entirely if the learner can demonstrate they already have mastery. That can be done either through prior learning assessment, formative testing, or mentor/manager assessment.

However as clear as it seems that competencies and learning should be connected, that is clearly not the case for many organizations. According to Brandon Hall Group’s Learning & Development Benchmarking study, 72% of companies have a system in place to manage competencies, and only 24% of them say there is a high level of integration with learning. In fact, 30% say there is actually no integration. If an organization cannot use L&D to address competencies and any gaps that may exist, exactly what are they managing? The Sciex Global Service Training Organization has recognized the importance of competency management and has tied competencies to each program and incorporated as part of the Career Daily Management Dashboard for managers to develop their associates.

Career Daily Management

Career Daily Management is a real-time dashboard that automatically pulls data from Oracle and the Career Portal. Accessing Career Daily Management is done by the use of single login through myDevelopment. It helps with the associate development plans that we all have with all of our direct reports to make sure that we understand where they want to go into business and how we can help them to get there. It is going to help you focus your training on particular needs both your needs as the area manager but also the needs of the associate to make sure that he or she is able to meet their career aspirations. Finally, it serves as a great aid for using in succession planning for key roles in your organization.

It is also important to know that the tool is not a replacement for the career portal and it is not a replacement for performance management. The dashboard is simply able to pull information and competencies and leadership anchors from Career Portal so that it is unified approach when looking as an associates performance or a teams’ skill set. It is not a performance rating tool however it is a great source of information that can be used when having performance discussions on working in upcoming projects.
